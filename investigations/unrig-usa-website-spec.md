### Hero

# The 20 Anti-Corruption Safeguards

**Subheadline:**
Not partisan. Not aspirational. Structural.

**Body:**
Each safeguard targets a specific leverage point in the system — the rules, information flows, feedback loops, and goals that determine how power operates. We don't argue about what the numbers should be. We change the structure that produces the numbers.

**Leverage point key:**
- **LP3** — Goals: What the system is actually optimizing for
- **LP4** — Self-organization: Who gets to participate and how
- **LP5** — Rules: The enforceable constraints on power
- **LP6** — Information flows: What the public is allowed to know
- **LP7-8** — Feedback loops: The mechanisms that correct (or fail to correct) abuse

---

### I. Democratic Structure & Electoral Integrity — LP4 (Self-Organization)

*These safeguards change who gets to participate and how.*

**Safeguard 1: Implement Ranked-Choice Voting Nationwide**
Replace winner-take-all elections with ranked-choice voting for all federal races. Voters rank candidates by preference; if no one wins a majority, the lowest-performing candidate is eliminated and their voters' second choices are counted.
*Structural function: Breaks the two-party lock on self-organization. Eliminates the spoiler effect. Rewards candidates who build broad coalitions instead of energizing narrow bases.*

**Safeguard 2: Enforce Term Limits for Congress**
Constitutional amendment limiting House members to 6 terms (12 years) and Senators to 2 terms (12 years).
*Structural function: Prevents the accumulation of structural power through incumbency. Refreshes the system's capacity for self-organization.*

**Safeguard 3: Establish Recall Elections for Federal Officials**
Constitutional amendment enabling citizens to recall any elected federal official through a petition and special election process, with signature thresholds scaled to constituency size.
*Structural function: Restores a negative feedback loop that currently doesn't exist at the federal level. Officials who violate public trust face consequences before their term ends.*

**Safeguard 4: End Partisan Gerrymandering**
Mandate independent redistricting commissions for all congressional districts. Commissions must use transparent criteria (population equality, geographic contiguity, community preservation) with public input and judicial review.
*Structural function: Removes the ability of those in power to choose their voters. Restores the feedback loop between elections and representation.*

**Safeguard 5: Expand and Protect Ballot Access**
Automatic voter registration at 18. National vote-by-mail option. Standardized early voting (minimum 14 days). Federal holiday for Election Day. Ban voter roll purges within 90 days of an election.
*Structural function: Removes structural barriers to self-organization. The system cannot self-correct if citizens cannot participate.*

---

### II. Money, Corruption & Power — LP5 (Rules) & LP3 (Goals)

*These safeguards change the rules governing money in politics and realign the system's goals.*

**Safeguard 6: Overturn Citizens United / Public Campaign Finance**
Constitutional amendment establishing that constitutional rights belong to natural persons, not corporations. Public financing system for federal campaigns. Contribution limits restored and indexed.
*Structural function: Changes the rules that allow unlimited money to function as speech. Disrupts the reinforcing loop where wealth → influence → rules → more wealth.*

**Safeguard 7: End Dark Budgeting & Secret Spending**
Full transparency for all federal spending above $10,000. Real-time public database of contracts, grants, and expenditures. Inspector General review of all classified budget items with declassification timelines.
*Structural function: LP6 intervention embedded in LP5 rules. You cannot have accountability without visibility. This is the Toxic Release Inventory principle applied to government spending.*

**Safeguard 8: Ban Elected Officials from Trading Individual Stocks**
Elected officials, their spouses, and dependent children must divest individual stock holdings into blind trusts or broad index funds within 90 days of taking office.
*Structural function: Eliminates the conflict of interest where legislators regulate industries they profit from. Changes the goal from personal enrichment to public service.*

---

### III. Accountability, Oversight & Legal Consequences — LP8 (Feedback Loops) & LP5 (Rules)

*These safeguards restore the self-correcting mechanisms that prevent abuse.*

**Safeguard 9: Strengthen Independent Oversight**
Statutory independence for Inspectors General — removable only for cause with 60-day congressional notification. Mandatory funding floors. Whistleblower protections with anti-retaliation enforcement.
*Structural function: Restores the negative feedback loops that detect and correct abuse. Oversight that can be fired by the people it oversees is not oversight.*

**Safeguard 10: Modernize Impeachment & Accountability Mechanisms**
Expedited impeachment timelines for clear constitutional violations. Independent counsel trigger for executive branch criminal conduct. Mandatory compliance with congressional subpoenas, enforceable by courts within 30 days.
*Structural function: Accelerates the feedback loop. The current system allows destruction at the speed of an executive order but correction at the speed of a committee hearing.*

**Safeguard 11: End Qualified Immunity**
Eliminate qualified immunity for government officials who violate constitutional rights. Citizens can seek damages in federal court. Government indemnification for good-faith actions; personal liability for willful violations.
*Structural function: Creates consequences for constitutional violations. Rules without enforcement are suggestions. This turns the rules back into rules.*

**Safeguard 12: Create a Civil Claims Department Against Government**
Independent federal agency to receive, investigate, and adjudicate civil claims against government misconduct. Streamlined process replacing the current requirement to sue the government in federal court.
*Structural function: Lowers the barrier to accountability. Most citizens cannot afford to sue the federal government. This creates an accessible feedback loop.*

---

### IV. Free, Fair & Independent Information — LP6 (Information Flows)

*These safeguards change what the public is allowed to know.*

**Safeguard 13: Establish Public Interest Reporting Standards**
Reinstate and modernize the Fairness Doctrine for broadcast and digital platforms. Require clear labeling of opinion vs. news content. Fund independent public media. Protect journalist shield laws at the federal level.
*Structural function: The Toxic Release Inventory proved that transparency alone — without enforcement — changes behavior. Apply the same principle to information. When the public can see what's happening, the system corrects itself.*

**Safeguard 14: Mandate Algorithmic Transparency & Accountability**
Platforms with over 10 million users must disclose recommendation algorithms, allow independent audits, and provide users with non-algorithmic feed options. Ban micro-targeted political advertising. Require disclosure of AI-generated content.
*Structural function: The information flow isn't just what's published — it's what's amplified. Algorithmic curation is an information flow. Making it visible is an LP6 intervention.*

---

### V. Budget & Spending Reform — LP5 (Rules) & LP8 (Feedback Loops)

*This safeguard changes the incentive structure of government spending.*

**Safeguard 15: End Use-It-or-Lose-It Budgeting**
Replace annual use-it-or-lose-it budget rules with rollover provisions. Agencies that spend efficiently retain savings for future use. Eliminate the perverse incentive to waste money at fiscal year-end.
*Structural function: The current rule punishes efficiency and rewards waste. Changing this single rule changes the goal of every budget manager in the federal government from "spend everything" to "spend wisely."*

---

### VI. Human Rights & Rule of Law — LP3 (Goals) & LP5 (Rules)

*These safeguards anchor the system's goals to international standards.*

**Safeguard 16: Ratify Outstanding International Human Rights Treaties**
Ratify the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination Against Women (CEDAW), the Convention on the Rights of the Child (CRC), and the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights (ICESCR).
*Structural function: Changes the system's reference goals. The US is the only UN member that hasn't ratified the CRC. Ratification creates enforceable standards that exist outside domestic political cycles.*

**Safeguard 17: Guarantee DOJ Independence**
Statutory firewall between the White House and DOJ criminal investigations. Special counsel triggered automatically for investigations involving the president, vice president, or cabinet members. Senate-confirmed DOJ Inspector General with independent budget.
*Structural function: The justice system cannot function as a feedback loop if the people being investigated control the investigators. This is the most fundamental rule-of-law safeguard.*

---

### VII. Economic Justice & Public Trust — LP5 (Rules) & LP6 (Information Flows)

*These safeguards address the structural conditions that erode public trust.*

**Safeguard 18: Mandate Cross-Agency Enforcement Coordination**
Federal agencies with overlapping jurisdiction (SEC, FTC, DOJ, CFPB, EPA) must share enforcement data, coordinate investigations, and report annually on cross-agency actions. Eliminate regulatory arbitrage.
*Structural function: Corporations currently exploit gaps between agencies. This closes the structural holes that allow powerful actors to evade accountability by falling between jurisdictions.*

**Safeguard 19: Establish Cost-of-Living Accountability**
Index minimum wage, Social Security, federal poverty guidelines, and congressional pay to a single, transparent cost-of-living metric updated annually. Automatic adjustments — no congressional vote required.
*Structural function: Removes the parameter-level fight entirely. Instead of arguing about what the minimum wage should be (LP12), this changes the rule (LP5) so the parameter adjusts automatically. The structure produces the right number.*

---

### VIII. Carceral System & Profit Incentives — LP3 (Goals)

*This safeguard changes what the carceral system optimizes for.*

**Safeguard 20: End Private Prisons & Profit from Incarceration**
Phase out all federal private prison contracts within 5 years. Ban for-profit immigration detention. Eliminate commissary price gouging and predatory communication fees. Redirect savings to reentry programs.
*Structural function: When incarceration is profitable, the system's goal becomes producing prisoners. This changes the goal from profit to rehabilitation. It is the clearest example of goal displacement in American governance.*

### The Action Ladder (cards — each rung builds on the last)

**Design note:** Visual ladder or stepped cards. Each rung has a name, a description, and a specific thing you can do this week. The ladder goes from personal to collective. You don't skip rungs.

**Rung 1: NAME IT**
*Start with the words.*
You just learned structural vocabulary — parameter failure, feedback loop failure, information capture. Use it. When someone says "everything is broken," offer the specific name. When you feel the weight at 2am, name it out loud. Names are search terms. Search terms build communities.
**This week:** Share one structural name with someone who needs it. Send them the article.

**Rung 2: CONNECT**
*Bridge two people who don't know they need each other.*
The most important connections in your life are not your closest friends — they're the weak ties, the friend-of-a-friend, the stranger who turns out to be carrying the same weight. The research (Granovetter, 1973; Burt, 2004) is clear: *bridging* between disconnected groups is more powerful than *bonding* within them.
**This week:** Introduce two people from different parts of your life who should know each other. One specific, personal introduction. "You're both thinking about X. I think you'd have a lot to talk about."

**Rung 3: ORGANIZE**
*Make the invisible community visible to itself.*
You probably know three people in your neighborhood who are all quietly dealing with the same problem. Say it out loud: "Have you noticed that several of us are dealing with this?" You're not organizing yet. You're making the community visible to itself. That is the step before organizing. Without it, nothing happens. (Wenger, 1998)
**This week:** Start or join a mutual aid network, a know-your-rights training, a community preparedness group, or a neighborhood association. If one exists, show up. If it doesn't, open your living room.

**This week:** Follow us. Share one post. Tag someone who needs to see it.

**Rung 6: PROTEST**
*Exercise your constitutional rights.*
Lawful protest is protected by the First Amendment. Know your rights before you go. Document what you see. Stay connected with your community. Protest is not just expression — it is a feedback loop. When the institutional feedback loops fail, the people become the feedback loop.

## CONTENT ENRICHMENT — RESEARCH FOUNDATIONS

### Voice & Approach
The site's voice draws from the justNICE blog series — specifically "You're Not Crazy" (naming-what-you-feel), "You Can't Google What You Can't Name" (vocabulary-is-infrastructure), and "How Isolated People Find Each Other" (connecting-isolated-voices). The core insight: validation first, then vocabulary, then connection, then action. Never the reverse.

### Meadows Leverage Points Framework
12 leverage points from least (LP12: parameters) to most effective (LP1: transcending paradigms). The Toxic Release Inventory as paradigmatic LP6 example. Safeguards target LP3-8.

### Hassan's BITE Model & Authoritarian Control
Quantitative validation: N=1,044, KMO .970, single factor "Authoritarian Control," alpha .982. Nonpartisan. Applies to any group. The Influence Continuum (Singer/Hassan) as the assessment tool.

### Connection Research
Granovetter (weak ties), Burt (structural holes — tertius iungens, not gaudens), Wenger (communities of practice — naming makes the community visible), adrienne maree brown (emergent strategy — "what happens between you and one other person"), Cortes/IAF (three genuine interactions before requesting anything), Horton/Highlander (cross-pollination IS the content), Straw (scene formation through internal circulation).

### The Pipeline
Felt sense → Name → Search term → Research trail → Community → Action. This is the connective tissue of the entire site. Every page is a stage in it. The navigation should make the arc visible.

### What Makes This Different
Three things no other good-government org does simultaneously:
1. Names the structural pattern (not just the individual abuses)
2. Gives citizens the vocabulary to see it themselves
3. Provides the connection infrastructure to find each other and act together

The three layers — institutional reform, structural literacy, personal & community defense — are the minimum viable response to a system that operates at all three levels. And the pipeline — feel it, name it, search it, find your people, act together — is the on-ramp that makes all three layers accessible to a person reading this at 2am on their phone.
